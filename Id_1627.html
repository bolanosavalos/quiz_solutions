Why "Fuse 2 Blown" results in 240V:

When Fuse 2 blows, look at what each probe is actually touching:

  • Left Probe (Line 2 Supply): This is on the "hot" side of the blown fuse. It is sitting directly on Phase B (120V) coming from the main breaker.
  • Right Probe (Line 1 Load side): This is on the load side of the healthy Fuse 1. Since Fuse 1 is still good, this probe is seeing Phase A (120V) coming through from Line 1.
  • The Measurement: The meter is measuring the difference between Phase B and Phase A. In a residential split-phase system, that is always 240V.

The Conclusion

If the diagram shows the probes on the Supply of Line 2 and the Load of Line 1:

  • A "120V" answer assumes an open circuit/no load.
  • A "0V" answer assumes Fuse 1 is blown (Backfeed).
  • A "240V" answer proves Fuse 2 is blown.
