The minimum bending radius for a 45 mm diameter, 5 kV portable power cable, based on the general requirements for portable (mining) cables (rated 5,000 volts or less) often cross-referenced with the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC) high-voltage rules, is 6 times the overall cable diameter.

Calculation

  1. Multiplier (from portable cable standard for 5 kV or less): 6
  2. Overall Cable Diameter (DD): 45 mm
  3. Minimum Bending Radius (RminR_{min}): Multiplier ×D\times D

Rmin=6×45 mm=270 mmR_{min} = 6 \times 45 \text{ mm} = 270 \text{ mm}

The minimum bending radius is 270 mm.
