Under the CEC, we refer to Rule 4-004 and Table 5C for ampacity correction factors.

For 6 current-carrying conductors in a single raceway, the calculation is as follows:

1. Identify the Correction Factor

According to CEC Table 5C, when 4 to 6 conductors are run in a common raceway or cable:

  • The correction factor is 0.80 (80%).

2. Perform the Calculation

To find the minimum ampacity required for the conductor before the table factor is applied, we divide the load current by the correction factor:

Calculated Ampacity=35 A0.80=43.75 A\text{Calculated Ampacity} = \frac{35\text{ A}}{0.80} = 43.75\text{ A}

3. Sizing the Conductor (CEC Table 2)

Under the CEC, you must also consider the Rule 4-006 regarding termination temperatures. Most modern breakers are rated for 75∘C75^\circ\text{C}.

Looking at Table 2 (for Copper):

  • 10 AWG is rated for 35A at 75∘C75^\circ\text{C}. However, 35 A×0.80=28 A35\text{ A} \times 0.80 = 28\text{ A}, which is too low for your 35A load.
  • 8 AWG is rated for 50A at 75∘C75^\circ\text{C}. Applying the factor: 50 A×0.80=40 A50\text{ A} \times 0.80 = 40\text{ A}.

Since 40A is greater than your 35A load, 8 AWG copper would be the minimum size required.

Key Summary

  • Calculated Current for Sizing: 43.75 Amps
  • CEC Table Used: Table 5C (80% factor)
  • Minimum Conductor Size: 8 AWG Copper (assuming 75∘C75^\circ\text{C} terminations).

A quick tip: If your load is considered a "continuous load" (running for more than 1 hour in some CEC jurisdictions or 3 hours in others), you may need to factor in an additional 125% per Rule 8-104.
