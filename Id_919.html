  • Aluminum conductors, 75°C ampacity values from your Table 4.
  • Ambient temperature 35°C → Table 5A factor at 75°C = 0.94.
  • 13 single-phase two-wire loads in the conduit → Table 5C factor for 13 conductors = 0.70.
  • Each load = 18 A, and the load is continuous → size for 125% of the load.
  • Combined correction factor = 0.94 \times 0.70 = 0.6580.94 × 0.70 = 0.658.

Continuous-load required conductor ampacity

Required ampacity (continuous) = 18 A × 1.25 = 22.5 A.

Because we must apply the correction factors to the conductor ampacity, the Table-4 (uncorrected) ampacity needed is: Required Table-4 ampacity = 22.5 A / 0.658 ≈ 34.19 A

Select conductor from Table 4 (75°C column)

From your Table 4 (75°C column):

  • AWG 10 (Al) = 30 A → too small.

Check derated ampacity of #8 Al: =  40 A × 0.658 = 26.32 A ≥ 22.5 A.

Conclusion

Use AWG #8 aluminum (75°C) conductors. With the ambient and multiple-conductor corrections applied, #8 Al provides a derated ampacity of ≈26.3 A, which satisfies the 125% continuous-load requirement for an 18 A continuous load.
