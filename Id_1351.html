To determine the horsepower of the 3-phase motor, we use the following electrical power formulas:

1. Calculate Input Power (PinP_{in})

For a three-phase system, the real input power in Watts (WW) is given by:     Pin=3×V×I×PFP_{in} = \sqrt{3} \times V \times I \times PF

Where:

  • V=600 VV = 600 \text{ V}
  • I=50 AI = 50 \text{ A}
  • PF=0.80PF = 0.80

Pin=3×600×50×0.80≈41,569.22 WP_{in} = \sqrt{3} \times 600 \times 50 \times 0.80 \approx 41,569.22 \text{ W}

2. Calculate Output Power (PoutP_{out})

The output power accounts for the efficiency (η\eta) of the motor:     Pout=Pin×ηP_{out} = P_{in} \times \eta

Where:

  • η=0.90\eta = 0.90

Pout=41,569.22×0.90≈37,412.30 WP_{out} = 41,569.22 \times 0.90 \approx 37,412.30 \text{ W}
