The maximum overload setting for a 10 HP, 600 V, three-phase motor is calculated based on its Full Load Current (FLC) according to Rule 28-306 of the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC).

1. Determine the Full Load Current (FLC)

The FLC is taken from CEC Table 44 (Full Load Current in Amperes of Three-Phase AC Motors). For a 10 HP motor operating on a 600 V system (which uses the 575 V column in the table):

  • FLC = 11.0 Amps

Note: If the motor nameplate provides a specific FLC, the nameplate value must be used instead of the CEC table value.

2. Apply the Overload Protection Factor

CEC Rule 28-306 specifies the maximum rating or trip setting for the motor's overload device:

  • If the Service Factor (SF) is 1.15 or greater or the motor has a marked temperature rise not exceeding 40∘C40^{\circ}\text{C}, the maximum setting is 125%125\% of the FLC.
  • If the Service Factor is less than 1.15 or is not marked (the default assumption when SF is unknown), the maximum setting is 115%115\% of the FLC.

Assuming the Service Factor is not marked/unknown, we use the 115%115\% factor:

3. Calculate the Maximum Overload Setting

Maximum Overload Setting=FLC×115%\text{Maximum Overload Setting} = \text{FLC} \times 115\% Maximum Overload Setting=11.0 A×1.15\text{Maximum Overload Setting} = 11.0 \text{ A} \times 1.15 Maximum Overload Setting=12.65 A\text{Maximum Overload Setting} = \mathbf{12.65 \text{ A}}

Therefore, the maximum overload protection device rating or trip setting for this motor is 12.65 Amps12.65 \text{ Amps}.
