  • Commutation is the process of turning a Thyristor (like an SCR or a Triac) OFF.
  • In an AC circuit, the current naturally drops to zero every half-cycle due to the alternating voltage.
  • A triac, once triggered into conduction, will naturally turn off when the load current drops below its holding current (IHI_H) and the AC voltage reverses, making the anode-to-cathode (or MT2-to-MT1) voltage negative for long enough.
  • This process, where the device turns off simply because the AC supply voltage reverses polarity and the current goes to zero, is called Natural Commutation or AC Line Commutation.
  • Natural commutation is designated as Class F commutation in standard classification schemes for thyristor-family devices.
