Dark Mode

In an industrial setting, managing power factor is a critical task for maintaining efficiency and avoiding utility surcharges.

Sample C of Q Exam Question

Question: An industrial facility is experiencing a low lagging power factor due to a high concentration of inductive loads from several large 600 V three-phase squirrel-cage motors. Which of the following methods would be the most effective for providing infinitely variable power factor correction while also performing mechanical work?

  • A) Installing a fixed bank of power factor correction capacitors in parallel with the main bus.
  • B) Connecting a synchronous motor and operating it in an over-excited state.
  • C) Replacing the existing induction motors with high-efficiency premium-rated induction motors.
  • D) Adding a series reactor to the feeder to reduce harmonic distortion.

Answer Key and Analysis

Correct Answer: B) Connecting a synchronous motor and operating it in an over-excited state.

Why this is the correct choice: A synchronous motor can act as a "synchronous condenser" when it is over-excited (meaning the DC field current is increased beyond the point of unity power factor). In this state, the motor supplies reactive power (kVARkVAR) to the system, which offsets the lagging kVARkVAR produced by induction motors. Unlike capacitors, which provide a fixed amount of correction, the amount of correction from a synchronous motor can be adjusted smoothly by changing the field excitation.

The "Distractor" (The "Common Mistake"): A) Installing a fixed bank of power factor correction capacitors. This is the answer most students gravitate toward because capacitors are the most common way to correct power factor in many textbooks. However, in the context of this specific question, capacitors do not "perform mechanical work," and a fixed bank cannot provide the "infinitely variable" control that an over-excited synchronous motor offers.

Technical Note for the Exam: When reviewing for the C of Q, remember that a synchronous motor at unity power factor does not provide correction; it must be over-excited to lead the current and provide the necessary correction for inductive (lagging) loads.
