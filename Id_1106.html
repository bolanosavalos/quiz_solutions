A transformer with a 5:1 voltage ratio and 200 secondary turns has 1000 primary turns.

Explanation

The relationship between the primary turns (Np\mathbf{N_p}), secondary turns (Ns\mathbf{N_s}), primary voltage (Vp\mathbf{V_p}), and secondary voltage (Vs\mathbf{V_s}) in an ideal transformer is given by the turns ratio equation:

VpVs=NpNs\frac{V_p}{V_s} = \frac{N_p}{N_s}

In this problem:

  • The voltage ratio (VpVs\frac{V_p}{V_s}) is 5:1\mathbf{5:1} (or 5\mathbf{5}).
  • The number of secondary turns (Ns\mathbf{N_s}) is 200\mathbf{200}.

Since NpNs=5\frac{N_p}{N_s} = 5, we can solve for the number of primary turns (Np\mathbf{N_p}):

Np=5×Ns\mathbf{N_p} = 5 \times \mathbf{N_s} Np=5×200\mathbf{N_p} = 5 \times 200 Np=1000\mathbf{N_p} = 1000

This is a step-down transformer because the primary voltage is higher than the secondary voltage (indicated by the ratio being greater than 1), which means it has more primary turns than secondary turns.
