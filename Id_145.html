Synchronous Motor Power Factor Control

The relationship between field excitation and power factor in a synchronous motor is often visualized using V-curves .

  • Under-Excited Field (Low IfI_f): The motor operates at a lagging power factor (like an induction motor or a simple inductor).
  • Normal Excitation: The motor operates at a unity power factor.
  • Over-Excited Field (High IfI_f): The motor operates at a leading power factor (like a capacitor).

This ability to operate at a leading power factor when overexcited is why synchronous motors are sometimes used specifically for power factor correction in industrial plants, in which case they are called synchronous condensers or compensators.

Why the Other Options Are Incorrect

  • a) adding a capacitor: While adding a static capacitor bank is the most common way to correct lagging power factor for inductive loads in general, the question specifically asks what you can do when troubleshooting a synchronous motor. The motor itself has the built-in capability to correct its own power factor through field excitation.
  • b) removing some load: Removing load will reduce the armature current but won't fundamentally change the motor's operating power factor at a given excitation level. If the motor is under-excited, it will remain lagging, just at a lower current.
  • c) under exciting the field: This is the cause of the lagging power factor, not the correction. It would make the power factor even more lagging.
