Rule 26-218: Overcurrent protection and ampacity of conductors for capacitors

The CEC specifies the requirements for conductors connecting a power-factor correcting capacitor to a motor circuit to ensure they can handle the current and any potential harmonic resonance. The rule breaks down as follows:

  • Minimum Ampacity: The conductors must have an ampacity of not less than one-third (1/31/3) the ampacity of the motor circuit conductors.
  • Capacitor Rating: Regardless of the motor circuit size, the conductors must also have an ampacity of at least 135% of the rated current of the capacitor.

Why 135%?

Capacitors are rated at 135% because they often operate at voltages slightly above their rating and are subject to harmonic currents. This "buffer" prevents the conductors from overheating under standard operating conditions where the capacitor might draw more than its nameplate current.
