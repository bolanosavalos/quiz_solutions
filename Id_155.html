Single-Phase Power Calculation Using PT and CT Readings

Given:

  • Service: 1200 V, 400 A, single-phase
  • PT: 1200 V primary, assumed 120 V secondary
  • CT: 400 A primary, assumed 5 A secondary
  • Ammeter reading on CT secondary: 3 A
  • Voltmeter reading on PT secondary: 80 V

We are asked to find single-phase power drawn by the load.

Step 1: PT Ratio and Primary Voltage

PT ratio=VprimaryVsecondary=1200120=10\text{PT ratio} = \frac{V_\text{primary}}{V_\text{secondary}} = \frac{1200}{120} = 10

Primary voltage corresponding to 80 V on PT secondary:

Vprimary=Vsecondary×PT ratio=80×10=800 VV_\text{primary} = V_\text{secondary} \times \text{PT ratio} = 80 \times 10 = 800\ \text{V}

✅ Primary voltage = 800 V

Step 2: CT Ratio and Primary Current

CT ratio=IprimaryIsecondary=4005=80\text{CT ratio} = \frac{I_\text{primary}}{I_\text{secondary}} = \frac{400}{5} = 80

Primary current corresponding to 3 A on CT secondary:

Iprimary=Isecondary×CT ratio=3×80=240 AI_\text{primary} = I_\text{secondary} \times \text{CT ratio} = 3 \times 80 = 240\ \text{A}

✅ Primary current = 240 A

Step 3: Single-Phase Power

P=Vprimary×Iprimary×PFP = V_\text{primary} \times I_\text{primary} \times \text{PF}

Assuming unity power factor (PF = 1):

P=800×240=192,000 W=192 kWP = 800 \times 240 = 192{,}000\ \text{W} = 192\ \text{kW}
