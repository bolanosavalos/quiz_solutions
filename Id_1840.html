To find the correct order of increasing total resistance, we can assign a value of R=1ΩR = 1 \Omega to each identical resistor and calculate the total resistance (RtR_t) for each circuit.

Circuit P

This consists of one resistor in series with a parallel bank of three resistors.

  • The three resistors in parallel: Rparallel = 111+11+11=13ΩR_{parallel} = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{1} + \frac{1}{1} + \frac{1}{1}} = \frac{1}{3} \Omega
  • Total resistance: Rtotal(P) =  1+13=1.33ΩR_{total(P)} = 1 + \frac{1}{3} = \mathbf{1.33 \Omega}

Circuit Q

This consists of two parallel branches, each containing two resistors in series.

  • Top branch: 1+1=2Ω1 + 1 = 2 \Omega

Circuit S

This consists of two resistors in series with a parallel bank of two resistors.

Comparing the calculated values:
