The maximum peak-to-peak output of a PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) waveform from an inverter with a 300 Volt DC supply is 600 Volts.

Explanation:

In a standard full-bridge (H-bridge) inverter, the DC supply voltage () is switched across the load in alternating polarities to create an AC-like waveform.

  1. Positive Cycle: The switches are configured to apply the full DC supply to the load, resulting in an instantaneous output of +300V .
  2. Negative Cycle: The switches reverse the polarity, applying the DC supply in the opposite direction, resulting in an instantaneous output of -300V.
