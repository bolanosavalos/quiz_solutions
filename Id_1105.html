A 60-cycle, eight-pole AC motor's synchronous speed is 900 revolutions per minute (RPM).

This speed is the theoretical maximum speed, calculated using the synchronous speed formula:

Ns=120×fPN_s = \frac{120 \times f}{P}

Where:

  • NsN_s is the synchronous speed in RPM.
  • ff is the AC power frequency in Hertz (Hz), which is 60 cycles/second or 60 Hz.
  • PP is the total number of poles, which is 8.

Calculation

Using the given values: Ns=120×608N_s = \frac{120 \times 60}{8} Ns=72008N_s = \frac{7200}{8} Ns=900 RPMN_s = 900 \text{ RPM}

Synchronous vs. Actual Speed

  • Synchronous Motors: A synchronous motor will run at exactly this speed (900 RPM900 \text{ RPM}) once it is locked into synchronization with the AC frequency.
  • Induction Motors: An induction motor (the most common type) will never quite reach the synchronous speed. It will run at a slightly lower speed, known as the full-load speed, due to a phenomenon called slip. The actual running speed of a typical 8-pole, 60 Hz induction motor under load is generally around 850 to 875 RPM.
