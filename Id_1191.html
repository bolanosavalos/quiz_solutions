To solve this, we use CEC Rule 28-108(1)(c), which explains how to size conductors for a group of motors with different duty cycles.

1. Identify Full Load Currents (Table 44)

For a 575V 3-phase system:

  • 50 HP (Continuous): 52 A
  • 25 HP (Continuous): 27 A
  • 40 HP (Intermittent): 41 A

2. Apply the CEC Multipliers

  • Largest Continuous Motor (50 HP): You must take 125% of this motor's FLC.

  • 52 A x 1.25 = 65 A

  • Other Continuous Motor (25 HP): You take this at 100%.

  • 27 A

  • Intermittent Motor (40 HP - 15 min): Per Table 27, a 15-minute intermittent duty motor uses an 85% multiplier.

  • 41 A x 0.85 = 34.85 A

3. Calculate Total Feeder Ampacity

Add the three values together:

  • 65 A + 27 A + 34.85 A = 126.85 A
