The requirements for hazardous locations, including the classification of areas like a sawmill with combustible dust, are covered in Section 18 of the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC).

Section 18: Hazardous Locations ðŸ“–

  • Scope: Section 18 outlines the supplementary requirements for the selection and installation of electrical equipment and wiring in hazardous locations.

  • Classification Basis (Rule 18-004): This section specifies that hazardous locations must be classified according to the nature of the hazard present:
    • Class I: Explosive gas or vapour atmospheres.
    • Class II: Explosive dust atmospheres (which applies to fine sawdust/wood dust).
    • Class III: Ignitable fibers or flyings.
  • Division/Zone: Within each Class, the section defines the Divisions (or Zones in the newer Zone system) based on the frequency and duration of the hazardous material's presence.

For this sawmill scenario (Class II, Division 1, Group G), the rules governing the proper electrical equipment, wiring methods, sealing requirements, and maintenance procedures would all be found within CEC Section 18.
