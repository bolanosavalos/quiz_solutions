Based on the provided Table 36A for aluminum conductor neutral supported cables, the minimum size of NS75 aluminum quadruplex cable that can be used to supply a 100 A100\text{ A} service is No. 2 AWG.

⚡ Conductor Sizing Determination

To select the minimum conductor size, the cable's ampacity must be equal to or greater than the 100 A100\text{ A} service requirement. We must look in the column for NS75 Quadruplex cable.

Phase Conductor Size (AWG) Ampacity, A (NS75 Quadruplex) Meets 100 A Requirement?
6 70 A70\text{ A} No
4 95 A95\text{ A} No
2 125 A125\text{ A} Yes
  • The No. 4 AWG cable has an ampacity of 95 A95\text{ A}, which is less than the 100 A100\text{ A} required.
  • The No. 2 AWG cable has an ampacity of 125 A125\text{ A}, which is the smallest standard size listed in the table that meets or exceeds the 100 A100\text{ A} requirement.

Therefore, the minimum size is No. 2 AWG.

ℹ️ Service and Cable Details

  • Service: 100 A100\text{ A}, 208 V208\text{ V} three-phase, four-wire.
  • Cable Type: Quadruplex (four-wire assembly for three-phase service) neutral supported cable, NS75 aluminum.
  • Rule Reference: The use of this table is as per Rule 4-004(5) of the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC).
  • Neutral Conductor: In a quadruplex NS75 cable, the neutral is an integral part of the assembly, and in this size range, it is typically the same gauge as the phase conductors (or sized to the required mechanical strength for support). By selecting No. 2 AWG for the phase conductors based on the ampacity, the entire cable assembly is rated correctly.
