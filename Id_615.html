The copper losses (Pcu\mathbf{P_{cu}}) in a transformer are proportional to the square of the load current, which is directly related to the load expressed as a percentage of the rated load.

The relationship is given by the formula:

Pcu,new=Pcu,rated×(New LoadRated Load)2\mathbf{P_{cu,new}} = \mathbf{P_{cu,rated}} \times \left( \frac{\text{New Load}}{\text{Rated Load}} \right)^2

In this problem:

  • Pcu,rated=22 watts\mathbf{P_{cu,rated}} = 22 \text{ watts}
  • The load is decreased to 75 percent75 \text{ percent}, so New LoadRated Load=0.75\frac{\text{New Load}}{\text{Rated Load}} = 0.75

Calculation

You can calculate the new copper losses by substituting the values into the formula.The copper losses when the load is decreased to 

75%75\% of the rated load would be 12.375 watts\mathbf{12.375 \text{ watts}}.

The full calculation is: Pcu,new=22 W×(0.75)2\mathbf{P_{cu,new}} = 22 \text{ W} \times (0.75)^2 Pcu,new=22 W×0.5625\mathbf{P_{cu,new}} = 22 \text{ W} \times 0.5625 Pcu,new=12.375 W\mathbf{P_{cu,new}} = 12.375 \text{ W}
