Based on the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC), here is the step-by-step calculation for the overcurrent protection (circuit breaker) size for three 1500W, 240V space heaters.

1. Calculate the Total Load

First, determine the total wattage and the resulting current (amperage):

  • Total Wattage: 3×1500W=4500W3 \times 1500\text{W} = 4500\text{W}
  • Total Current (II): I=PV=4500W240V=18.75AI = \frac{P}{V} = \frac{4500\text{W}}{240\text{V}} = 18.75\text{A}

2. Apply Continuous Load Factors

According to CEC Rule 8-104, electric heating loads are considered continuous loads (loads expected to continue for more than 1 hour in a 2-hour period).

The overcurrent device (OCD) must be rated at 125% of the continuous load (unless using a 100%-rated breaker, which is uncommon for residential applications):

  • Minimum Overcurrent Rating: 18.75A×1.25=23.4375A18.75\text{A} \times 1.25 = 23.4375\text{A}

3. Determine the Overcurrent Protection Size

Since the minimum required rating is 23.44A, you must select the next standard size breaker.

  • Standard Breaker Size: 25A or 30A.
  • In practice, a 30A 2-pole breaker is the most common and widely available choice for this load.

4. Check Grouping Rules (Section 62)

According to CEC Rule 62-114:

  • In residential occupancies, two or more heating devices may be connected to a single branch circuit, provided the circuit rating does not exceed 30A.
  • Since the required circuit size for these three heaters is within the 30A limit, they can all be placed on the same circuit.

Summary

  • Total Current: 18.75A18.75\text{A}
  • Minimum Breaker Size: 25A (or more commonly 30A)
  • Conductor Size: If using a 30A breaker, you would typically use #10 AWG copper wire.
