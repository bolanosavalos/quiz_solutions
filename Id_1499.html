To calculate the minimum conductor size for the feeder, we look at Rule 28-108 of the CEC 2024. This rule ensures the wire can handle the continuous load of all motors plus the extra heat generated during the startup of the largest motor.

1. The Calculation (Rule 28-108)

The rule states that the feeder ampacity must be at least:

  • 125% of the Full Load Current (FLC) of the largest motor.
  • Plus the sum of the FLC of all other motors in the group.

The Math:

  • Largest Motor (30 A30\text{ A}): 30 A×1.25=37.5 A30\text{ A} \times 1.25 = \mathbf{37.5\text{ A}}
  • Other Motors: 22 A+15 A=37 A22\text{ A} + 15\text{ A} = \mathbf{37\text{ A}}
  • Total Required Ampacity: 37.5 A+37 A=74.5 A37.5\text{ A} + 37\text{ A} = \mathbf{74.5\text{ A}}
