To solve this problem, we need to apply the principle of conservation of charge. When you connect a charged capacitor to an uncharged one, the total initial charge is redistributed across both capacitors until they reach a common voltage.

1. Identify the Givens

  • Capacitor 1 (C1C_1): 2 μF2\ \mu\text{F}
  • Initial Voltage of C1C_1 (V1V_1): 150 V150\ \text{V}
  • Capacitor 2 (C2C_2): 4 μF4\ \mu\text{F}
  • Initial Voltage of C2C_2 (V2V_2): 0 V0\ \text{V} (it is uncharged)

2. Calculate Initial Total Charge (QtotalQ_{\text{total}})

Charge is defined by the formula Q=C×VQ = C \times V. Since only the first capacitor has a charge initially:

Qtotal=C1×V1Q_{\text{total}} = C_1 \times V_1

Qtotal=2 μF×150 V=300 μCQ_{\text{total}} = 2\ \mu\text{F} \times 150\ \text{V} = 300\ \mu\text{C}

3. Calculate Total Capacitance (CtotalC_{\text{total}})

When capacitors are connected in parallel, their capacitances simply add up:

Ctotal=C1+C2C_{\text{total}} = C_1 + C_2

Ctotal=2 μF+4 μF=6 μFC_{\text{total}} = 2\ \mu\text{F} + 4\ \mu\text{F} = 6\ \mu\text{F}

4. Find the Final Common Voltage (VcommonV_{\text{common}})

Now, we use the total charge and the total capacitance to find the new voltage across the combination:

Vcommon=QtotalCtotalV_{\text{common}} = \frac{Q_{\text{total}}}{C_{\text{total}}}

Vcommon=300 μC6 μF=50 VV_{\text{common}} = \frac{300\ \mu\text{C}}{6\ \mu\text{F}} = 50\ \text{V}

Final Answer:

The voltage existing on the combination is 50 volts.
