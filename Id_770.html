The correct answer is b) open the circuit.

When a thermal relay detects that a unit is using too much current (an overload condition), it is designed to trip and open the control circuit.

Here's why:

  • Overload →\rightarrow Heat: Excessive current generates too much heat within the relay (usually through a bimetallic strip or melting alloy).
  • Trip Mechanism →\rightarrow Open Circuit: This heat activates a mechanical tripping mechanism. The relay's auxiliary contact, which is wired in the motor's control circuit, will then open (or change state from normally closed to open).
  • Control Circuit Interrupt →\rightarrow Power Off: By opening the control circuit, it de-energizes the main contactor coil, which in turn causes the main power contacts to open, thus cutting power to the overloaded unit (like a motor).
