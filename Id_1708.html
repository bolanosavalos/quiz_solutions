To find the output of an open-delta (V-V) transformer bank, we first need to determine the capacity of the individual transformers and then apply the derating factor required for this specific configuration.

Based on Canadian electrical standards and general transformer theory, here is the breakdown:

1. Calculate the capacity of one transformer

First, find the kVA rating of a single transformer in the bank: kVAsingle=Voltage×Amperage1000kVA_{single} = \frac{Voltage \times Amperage}{1000}

kVAsingle=480 V×400 A1000=192 kVAkVA_{single} = \frac{480\text{ V} \times 400\text{ A}}{1000} = 192\text{ kVA}

2. Calculate the Open Delta capacity

An open-delta bank uses only two transformers to provide three-phase power. However, because the current in the windings is 30∘30^\circ out of phase with the line voltage, the bank cannot operate at the simple sum of the two transformers (192×2=384 kVA192 \times 2 = 384\text{ kVA}).

Instead, the capacity of an open-delta bank is 57.7% of the capacity of a full three-transformer delta bank, or more simply, 86.6% of the combined capacity of the two remaining transformers:

Output=3×kVAsingleOutput = \sqrt{3} \times kVA_{single}

Output=1.732×192 kVAOutput = 1.732 \times 192\text{ kVA}

Output≈332.5 kVAOutput \approx 332.5\text{ kVA}

Correcting the Premise

Looking at the options provided (A through D), there is a common instructional "trick" or misunderstanding often found in trade exams regarding whether the "400 amps" refers to the bank or the individual transformer.

If we calculate based on the total capacity of a full delta bank (192 kVA×3=576 kVA192\text{ kVA} \times 3 = 576\text{ kVA}) and then take the 57.7% ratio: 576 kVA×0.577=332.4 kVA576\text{ kVA} \times 0.577 = 332.4\text{ kVA}
