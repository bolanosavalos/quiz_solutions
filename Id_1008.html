Equivalent Resistance Calculation

To find the resistance between any two terminals (for example, A and B), you must identify the two parallel paths:

  1. Direct Path: Resistor RABR_{AB} connects directly between the two terminals.
  2. Indirect Path: Resistors RACR_{AC} and RCBR_{CB} are connected in series with each other.

Using the textbook notation we discussed:

  • Series Path: 5+5=10 Ω5 + 5 = 10\,\Omega
  • Equivalent Resistance (ReqR_{eq}): 5∥(5+5)5 \parallel (5 + 5)

Req=5×105+10=5015≈3.33 ΩR_{eq} = \frac{5 \times 10}{5 + 10} = \frac{50}{15} \approx 3.33\,\Omega

In this configuration, the equivalent resistance across any two vertices is always 3.33 Ω3.33\,\Omega.
