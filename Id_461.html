  • a) Split Bolt Connection: A split bolt can be used for copper-to-aluminum connections, but only if it is specifically listed for the purpose (CU/AL rated) and an anti-oxidant joint compound is applied.
  • b) Compression Lug Connection: These are commonly used for large copper-to-aluminum wires, but they must be dual-rated (CU/AL) and used with an anti-oxidant compound.

  • d) Bimetallic or Tined Metal Strips: These are actually key components of approved connections. Bimetallic strips (like those found in certain connectors) or terminals with a tin plating are designed to prevent the direct, corrosive contact of raw copper and raw aluminum.

Best Answer Choice: c) terminal blocks

While all the listed connections can be used if they are specifically CU/AL rated, standard, non-rated terminal blocks are the most common example in electrical work of a connection device that is typically not approved for aluminum-to-copper wiring.

The general rule is that any device or connector not explicitly marked CU/AL or CO/ALR is not approved for connecting copper and aluminum conductors. Standard terminal blocks often fall into this unrated category.
