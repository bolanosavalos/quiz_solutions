The color coding system for resistors uses bands of color to indicate the resistance value, tolerance, and sometimes the temperature coefficient. The number of bands can vary (typically 4, 5, or 6), but the color-to-value assignments generally follow a standard chart.

4-Band Resistor (Most Common)

The four bands are read from left to right, usually with the tolerance band (Gold or Silver) at the far right or indicated by a wider space before it.

  1. Band 1: 1st Significant Digit
  2. Band 2: 2nd Significant Digit
  3. Band 3: Multiplier (the power of 10 to multiply the first two digits by)
  4. Band 4: Tolerance (the percentage of possible variation)
