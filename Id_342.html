Explanation

The instantaneous voltage (ν\nu) for a sinusoidal AC circuit is determined by the equation: ν=Vpeaksin⁡(θ)\nu = V_{peak} \sin(\theta) where:

  • VpeakV_{peak} is the peak voltage (or maximum voltage).
  • θ\theta is the phase angle (in radians or degrees).

1. Find the Peak Voltage (VpeakV_{peak})

An AC voltmeter reads the Root Mean Square (RMS) voltage (VrmsV_{rms}). The relationship between RMS voltage and peak voltage for a sine wave is: Vpeak=Vrms×2V_{peak} = V_{rms} \times \sqrt{2}
