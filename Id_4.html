Rule 6-304 specifically addresses the construction type for this cable, not its ultimate minimum ampere rating.

Based only on the text of Rule 6-304(1)(b) that you provided:

The minimum size of single-conductor aluminum-sheathed cable that can be used for a consumer's service is one that is larger than No. 4 AWG aluminum.

Minimum Conductor Size

The conductor must be a size:

Conductor Size>No. 4 AWG Aluminum\text{Conductor Size} > \text{No. 4 AWG Aluminum}

The next standard conductor size immediately larger than No. 4 AWG is No. 3 AWG. Therefore, the minimum size permitted by this specific rule for single-conductor aluminum-sheathed cable is No. 3 AWG.

AWG Size Condition (Rule 6-304(1)(b))
No. 4 Not permitted (must be larger than No. 4)
No. 3 Permitted (is larger than No. 4)

Context of Other Rules

  • Service Minimum Size (Rule 6-302/6-304): Historically, Rule 6-302 or similar rules for overhead service conductors established a minimum size of No. 2 AWG aluminum to provide mechanical strength and adequate ampacity (unless a smaller 60 A service size is permitted by other rules, which would use No. 3 AWG aluminum).

Conclusion: When considering Rule 6-304(1)(b) in isolation, the minimum permitted size is No. 3 AWG aluminum. However, in a real-world CEC application, you must also satisfy the minimum size requirements specified elsewhere in Section 6 (like the historical minimum of No. 2 AWG for exposed service conductors, unless a 60 A service is permitted).
