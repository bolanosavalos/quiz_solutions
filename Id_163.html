In the context of paralleling two alternators, a synchroscope is a device used to indicate whether the two alternators are synchronized in terms of frequency and phase before they are connected to the same bus or grid. This is crucial because improper synchronization can cause equipment damage or operational issues.

What the Synchroscope Indicates:

  1. Phase Difference: The synchroscope shows the phase relationship between the voltages of the two alternators. It indicates whether the two alternators are in phase with each other.
  2. Speed (Frequency) Difference: It also helps to identify if the alternators are running at the same speed, meaning their frequencies are matching. The faster the needle moves, the greater the difference in frequency.
  3. Correct Timing for Connection: When the alternators are synchronized, the needle of the synchroscope will point to the "zero" mark (or sometimes a neutral point), indicating that the alternatorsâ€™ voltages are in phase and they are running at the same frequency.

Operation:

  • If the synchroscope needle is moving slowly, it means the two alternators are running at nearly the same speed, but one may be slightly faster or slower than the other.
  • If the needle is stationary or moves erratically, the two alternators are not synchronized, and connecting them could cause damage.

CEC (Canadian Electrical Code) Considerations:

While the CEC itself may not specifically discuss synchroscopes, the overall safety and proper operation of electrical equipment, including generators and alternators, are covered under general safety standards. Ensuring that alternators are properly synchronized before paralleling is essential to avoid faults and ensure the system runs reliably and safely.

Paralleling alternators without proper synchronization can lead to:

  • Mechanical stress on the generators.
  • Electrical surges that may damage sensitive equipment.
  • Bus bar faults if there is a significant difference in voltage or phase angle.

Why a Synchroscope is Important:

In practical terms, the synchroscope is an essential tool in ensuring safe operation when paralleling alternators or generators. It is commonly used in power plants, large industrial settings, or any installation where multiple power sources need to work together.
