The output voltage changed by the equivalent of one Least Significant Bit (LSB) step.

Since the input changed from 100 1102100\ 110_2 to 100 1112100\ 111_2, the change is only in the LSB, which is a change of 1 in the digital input value.

The magnitude of this change in voltage (ΔV\Delta V), also known as the resolution or step size, is calculated as:

ΔV=Vfull-scale2n−1\Delta V = \frac{V_{\text{full-scale}}}{2^n - 1}

Where:

  • Vfull-scaleV_{\text{full-scale}} is the maximum output voltage (10 V10\ \text{V}).
  • nn is the number of bits (66).
