Why Point X has 4 Wires (Diagram 9)

In this specific schematic, point X is a 3-way switch (S3). In a standard Canadian residential setup for a 3-way system where the switch is at the end of a run or branched off, the wire count at that box is 4.

Here is the breakdown of the conductors:

  1. Neutral (White): Required at the switch location by the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC Rule 4-022).
  2. Traveler 1: One of the two wires that connect the two 3-way switches.
  3. Traveler 2: The second wire that connects the two 3-way switches.
  4. Common (Pivot): The wire that either takes the "Line" power or acts as the "Switched Leg" to the light.
