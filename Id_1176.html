That is an excellent comparative question that highlights a key exception in neutral sizing rules!

You are asking why the neutral demand load calculation for the 300 A300 \text{ A} feeder does not include the 70%70\% reduction when there is 100 A100 \text{ A} of discharge lighting.

The Answer: The Exception for Non-Linear Loads (Discharge Lighting)

The reason you cannot apply the 70%70\% derating (reduction) is because discharge lighting is a type of non-linear load.

The Canadian Electrical Code (CEC) Rule 4-018 that allows the reduction (first 200 A200 \text{ A} at 100%100\%, remainder at 70%70\%) has an important exception:

  • CEC Rule 4-018(1)(a)(ii) (for 3-wire feeders) states that the reduction shall not be applied to any portion of the neutral current that is supplied to discharge lighting, data processing, or other non-linear loads where the neutral current is subject to harmonic currents.

Non-linear loads create harmonic currents (specifically, the third harmonic, or triplen harmonics). Instead of cancelling out in the neutral conductor (which happens in a balanced 180∘180^\circ system like 240/120 V240/120 \text{ V}), these harmonic currents add up on the neutral. This can cause the neutral conductor to carry a current equal to, or even greater than, the phase conductors, even if the load is perfectly balanced.

Corrected Steps for Calculation

Since there is discharge lighting, you must assume the neutral carries 100%100\% of the maximum unbalanced load, including the portion supplied to the non-linear loads.

  1. Determine Maximum Unbalanced Load:

    • L1L_1 Load: 200 A200 \text{ A}
    • L2L_2 Load: 300 A300 \text{ A}
    • The neutral must be sized for the maximum phase current: 300 A\mathbf{300 \text{ A}}

  2. Determine Non-Linear Load Component:

    • Discharge Lighting: 100 A100 \text{ A}

  3. Apply CEC Rule 4-018 (with Non-Linear Exception):

    • Because 100 A100 \text{ A} of the load is non-linear, the 70%70\% reduction cannot be applied to that 100 A100 \text{ A} portion.
    • The most conservative approach, which is required by the Code when non-linear loads are present, is to size the neutral conductor for 100%100\% of the maximum phase current (300 A). The code essentially treats the entire load as potentially unbalanced and non-reducible due to the presence of harmonics.

  4. Total Neutral Demand Load: INeutral Demand=300 A\mathbf{I_{\text{Neutral Demand}} = 300 \text{ A}}
