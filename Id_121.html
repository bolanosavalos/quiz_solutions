The maximum size for a solid copper conductor allowed in a raceway under the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC) is No. 10 AWG (American Wire Gauge) copper.

For conductors larger than No. 10 AWG, the CEC generally requires them to be stranded rather than solid, particularly when installed in raceways (conduits, tubing, etc.). This requirement is primarily due to the mechanical difficulty of pulling large solid conductors and the damage that can occur when bending them.

CEC Requirement for Solid Conductors

The general principle in the Canadian Electrical Code (and similar codes) is that as the conductor size increases, it must be stranded for ease of installation and to prevent damage.

  • Maximum Solid Size (General): The largest size generally permitted to be solid is No. 10 AWG copper.
  • Minimum Stranded Size (General): Conductors of size No. 8 AWG and larger are generally required to be stranded when installed in a raceway.

Examples of Conductor Sizes

Here are four examples of copper conductor sizes and their common solid/stranded application under the CEC:

Conductor Size (AWG) Solid or Stranded (Typical Use) Reason/Comment
No. 14 AWG Solid or Stranded Common for lighting and general receptacle circuits (15A). Usually solid for easier termination.
No. 12 AWG Solid or Stranded Common for general receptacle circuits (20A). Typically solid for easier termination, but stranded is available.
No. 10 AWG Solid or Stranded The largest size generally permitted to be solid when installed in a raceway.
No. 8 AWG Stranded (Typically) Generally required to be stranded when installed in a raceway due to stiffness.

Appropriate Size Selection (No. 10 AWG)

Based on the general rule for conductors in raceways, the appropriate example that represents the largest permitted solid copper conductor size is:

No. 10 AWG\text{No. 10 AWG}

  • While smaller sizes like No. 14 AWG and No. 12 AWG are also permitted to be solid, the No. 10 AWG is the largest copper conductor size that can generally be manufactured and installed as a solid conductor in a raceway according to the principle of the code. Larger sizes are nearly always stranded.
