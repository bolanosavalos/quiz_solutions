Impact of Extra-Low Voltage Factor (Note 4)

CEC 2024 Table D3 Note 4 (or an equivalent note from an Appendix) is specifically designed for ELV systems and instructs the user to apply a modification factor to the KK-value (resistance) when the circuit involves a DC voltage of 60 V or less (which 6 V6 \text{ V} clearly is).

The purpose of this factor is to account for the difference between the AC resistance values (which include skin effect and proximity effect, built into the standard Table D3 KK-factors) and the true DC resistance of the conductor. For ELV DC circuits, the true DC resistance provides a more accurate calculation.

If the note specifies a multiplication factor of 0.860.86, we must apply it to the KK-value before calculating the current.

1. Determine the Corrected Resistance (KDCK_{DC})

  • Standard KK-factor for #8 AWG Copper (75∘C75^\circ\text{C}): KAC≈2.54ohmkmK_{AC} \approx 2.54 \frac{\text{ohm}}{\text{km}}
  • Extra-Low Voltage Factor (Note 4): 0.860.86
  • Corrected KK-factor (KDCK_{DC}): KDC=KAC×0.86K_{DC} = K_{AC} \times 0.86 KDC=2.54ohmkm×0.86≈2.1844ohmkmK_{DC} = 2.54 \frac{\text{ohm}}{\text{km}} \times 0.86 \approx 2.1844 \frac{\text{ohm}}{\text{km}}
