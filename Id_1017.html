To determine the correct wire size for a motor, we have to look at the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC) rules. Specifically, Rule 28-106 requires that the conductors feeding a single motor have an ampacity of at least 125% of the motor's Full Load Amperage (FLA).

Selecting the Wire Size

While RW90 insulation is rated for 90∘C90^\circ\text{C}, Rule 4-006 generally requires us to use the 75∘C75^\circ\text{C} column unless the equipment and terminations are specifically marked for 90∘C90^\circ\text{C}.

  • At the 75∘C75^\circ\text{C} Column (Standard Practice):
    • #3 AWG is rated for 100 A (Too small).
    • #2 AWG is rated for 115 A.
  • At the 90∘C90^\circ\text{C} Column (If terminations allow):
    • #3 AWG is rated for 115 A.

Recommendation

For a standard installation where termination temperatures are limited to 75∘C75^\circ\text{C}, you should use #2 AWG Copper RW90.
