The Canadian Electrical Code (CEC) Rule 32-100(4) specifies the minimum insulation rating for conductors used in fire alarm systems, which would typically cover smoke detectors in a plant environment.

The minimum insulation rating required for the conductors is 300 Volts.

This requirement is for the insulation of the wiring itself, which must be rated for at least 300 V, though the operating voltage of the circuit may be lower (such as 24V for a typical fire alarm system or 120V for a stand-alone smoke alarm).

Additional Context (CEC Rule 32-100)

  • Rule 32-100 covers the requirements for insulated conductors and cables in fire alarm systems.
  • The system for a plant is classified as a Fire Alarm System, which has specific wiring requirements under Section 32 of the CEC, unlike a residential smoke alarm.
  • The conductor requirements also typically mandate copper material and an ampacity adequate to carry the maximum circuit current.

This rule ensures that the wiring used for critical life safety systems meets a baseline for durability and safety within the building's electrical environment.
