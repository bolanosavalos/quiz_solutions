CEC 2021 Table 9G provides the 40% allowable cross-sectional area for various standard sizes of Electrical Metallic Tubing (EMT).

In the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC), the conduit fill rules (Rule 12-910) require that when installing more than two insulated conductors in a raceway, the total cross-sectional area of all conductors must not exceed 40% of the internal cross-sectional area of the raceway.

How Table 9G is Used in a Calculation

Table 9G is the final step in a conduit fill calculation for EMT. You follow these steps for ≥3\ge 3 conductors:

  1. Find Conductor Area: Use CEC Table 10A (for stranded) or 10C (for solid) to find the cross-sectional area (in mm2\text{mm}^2) of a single conductor based on its size and insulation type (e.g., R90 XLPE).

  2.   Calculate Total Area: Multiply the single conductor area by the total number of conductors to get the total required area.          Total required area= 28.17x13 = 366.21 mm2

  3.  Select Conduit Size: Find the smallest trade size in Table 9G (EMT) where the Allowable Area at 40% Fill is greater than or equal to your calculated Total Area.

For example, to determine the conduit size for 1313 #8 AWG R90 XLPE conductors (which you calculated as 366.21 mm2366.21 \text{ mm}^2 total area):
