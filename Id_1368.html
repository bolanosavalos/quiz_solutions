Based on the Canadian Electrical Code (CSA C22.1) 26-250 1) & 2), specifically the rules for High Voltage installations (over 750 V), here is the required fuse size for your transformer.


1. Calculate Rated Primary Current (IprimaryI_{primary})

First, determine the full-load current for the primary side (4160 V).

Iprimary=kVA10003VprimaryI_{primary} = \frac{\text{kVA} \times 1000}{\sqrt{3} \times V_{primary}}

Iprimary=350,0001.732416048.57 AI_{primary} = \frac{350,000}{1.732 \times 4160} \approx \mathbf{48.57 \text{ A}}

2. Determine CEC Protection Requirements

Since the primary voltage is 4160 V (exceeding 750 V), Rule 26-250 and Table 50 apply.

Transformer Data:

  • Impedance (%Z): 5% (This falls under the "Not more than 7.5%" category in Table 50).
  • Device Type: Fuse.

Rule 26-250(1)(a) Primary Protection Only: If you do not have coordinated secondary overcurrent protection, the primary fuse must be rated at not more than 150% of the rated primary current.

Calculation: 48.57 A1.50=72.86 A48.57 \text{ A} \times 1.50 = \mathbf{72.86 \text{ A}}

3. Select Standard Fuse Size

Rule 26-250(2) states that if the calculated value (150%) does not correspond to a standard rating of a fuse, the next higher standard rating shall be permitted.

  • Calculated Max: 72.86 A
  • Standard Medium Voltage Fuse Sizes (E-Rated): Common ratings in this range are 50E, 65E, 80E, 100E.
  • Selection: The next standard size above 72.86 A is 80 A (or 80E).

Recommended Primary Fuse: 80 A (Rated for 5 kV or higher)

Important Note on Secondary Protection

If you have overcurrent protection on the secondary side rated at not more than 150% of the secondary current (as per Rule 26-250(4) and Table 50), you are permitted to increase the primary fuse size up to 300% of the rated primary current.

  • With Secondary Protection: 48.57 A3.00=145.7 A48.57 \text{ A} \times 3.00 = 145.7 \text{ A} \rightarrow 150 A Fuse.

Unless you confirm secondary protection is installed and coordinated, you must adhere to the 80 A limit for primary-only protection.
