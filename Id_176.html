In the world of electrical controls, timing relays are categorized by how they behave once the control signal (the coil) is applied or removed. Here is the breakdown of why TDOE is the standard label for this specific function:

Understanding the Acronyms

  • TDOE (Time Delay On Energization): These contacts wait for a specific amount of time after the coil is energized before they change state (Open to Closed, or Closed to Open).
  • TDOD (Time Delay On De-energization): These contacts change state immediately when the coil is energized, but wait for a set time after the coil is turned off before returning to their normal state.

Common Industry Labels

While TDOE is the formal functional description, you will often see these terms used interchangeably in manuals and on schematics:

Term Meaning Application
On-Delay Same as TDOE Used for staggered motor starting to prevent power surges.
Off-Delay Same as TDOD Used for cooling fans that must run after a machine is turned off.
NOTC Normally Open, Timed Closed A specific type of TDOE contact.
NCTC Normally Closed, Timed Open Another specific type of TDOE contact.

Note on TDRE/TDRD: Options C and D are generally not standard industry acronyms for contact labeling, though "TDR" is often used as a general abbreviation for the Time Delay Relay device itself.
