Method 1: Using the Three-Phase Power Formula

The formula for total power in a balanced three-phase system is:  P=3×VLL×IL×PFP = \sqrt{3} \times V_{LL} \times I_L \times \text{PF}

Method 2: Calculating Per Phase (Standard for 4-wire systems)

In a balanced 4-wire system, the total power is distributed equally across the three phases. Since lighting loads are typically connected line-to-neutral (120 V120\text{ V}):      Power per phase=60,000 W3=20,000 W\text{Power per phase} = \frac{60,000\text{ W}}{3} = 20,000\text{ W}
