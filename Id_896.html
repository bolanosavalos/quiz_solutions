Understanding Voltage Regulation

The percent voltage regulation (%VR\%VR) of an alternator (or synchronous generator) is a measure of the change in its terminal voltage when the full load is removed, while the speed and field excitation remain constant. It's essentially an indicator of how stable the output voltage is under varying load conditions.

The terms in the formula stand for:

  • NLV = No-Load Terminal Voltage (VNLV_{NL})
  • FLV = Full-Load Terminal Voltage (VFLV_{FL})

The formula calculates the difference between the no-load voltage and the full-load voltage, divided by the no-load voltage, all multiplied by 100 to express it as a percentage.

%VR=VNL−VFLVFL×100\%VR = \frac{V_{NL} - V_{FL}}{V_{FL}} \times 100

Wait! The standard definition of voltage regulation for most electrical power sources (including alternators and transformers) is the voltage difference divided by the full-load voltage (VFLV_{FL}).
