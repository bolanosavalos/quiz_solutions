To determine the correct wire size for a 19.75 kW range under the 2024 Canadian Electrical Code (CEC), we need to apply demand factors and then select the conductor based on the specific conditions you provided.

1. Calculate the Demand Load

According to Section 8 (Circuit loading and demand factors), we don't size the circuit for the full 19.75 kW. For a single household range, Table 8 (or Rule 8-300) typically applies:

  • First 12 kW: Demand is 8 kW.
  • Remainder: For any rating above 12 kW, add 40% of the amount by which the rating exceeds 12 kW.

The Math:

  • Excess: 19.75 kW−12 kW=7.75 kW19.75\text{ kW} - 12\text{ kW} = 7.75\text{ kW}
  • 40% of Excess: 7.75 kW×0.40=3.1 kW7.75\text{ kW} \times 0.40 = 3.1\text{ kW}
  • Total Demand: 8 kW+3.1 kW=11.1 kW8\text{ kW} + 3.1\text{ kW} = \mathbf{11.1\text{ kW}}

2. Determine Current (Amperage)

Assuming a standard residential single-phase voltage of 240V:

I=PV=11,100 W240 V=46.25 AI = \frac{P}{V} = \frac{11,100\text{ W}}{240\text{ V}} = \mathbf{46.25\text{ A}}

3. Select Conductor Size (NMD90)

Even though NMD90 is rated for 90°C, we must look at Table 2 and consider your equipment termination temperature.

  • Termination Temperature: You specified 90°C terminations. This is a significant detail, as most standard residential breakers are only rated for 60°C or 75°C. Since you've confirmed 90°C terminations, we can use the 90°C column of Table 2.
  • Current Required: 46.25 A.
