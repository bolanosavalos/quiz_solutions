The synchronous speed of a 50 Hz, four-pole squirrel cage induction motor is 1500 RPM (Revolutions Per Minute).

The synchronous speed (NsN_s) is the speed of the rotating magnetic field within the motor's stator and is calculated using the formula:

Ns=120×fPN_s = \frac{120 \times f}{P}

Where:

  • NsN_s is the synchronous speed in revolutions per minute (RPM)
  • ff is the AC power supply frequency in Hertz (Hz)
  • PP is the number of poles

Calculation

Given:

  • Frequency (ff) = 50 Hz
  • Number of Poles (PP) = 4

Ns=120×504N_s = \frac{120 \times 50}{4} Ns=60004N_s = \frac{6000}{4} Ns=1500 RPMN_s = 1500 \text{ RPM}

Important Note

It is important to remember that an induction motor's actual rotor speed is always slightly less than its synchronous speed due to a phenomenon called slip. For this motor, the full-load speed would be slightly below 1500 RPM (e.g., 1450 RPM).
