Calculation

The percent impedance (%Z)(\%Z) of a transformer is defined as the ratio of the impedance voltage (Vz)(V_z) (the voltage required to circulate rated current with the low-voltage side short-circuited) to the rated voltage (Vrated)(V_{\text{rated}}) of the winding the short-circuit test is performed on, expressed as a percentage. Since the short-circuit test is typically performed on the high-voltage (HV) side, we use the HV rated voltage.

The formula is: %Z=VzVrated×100\%Z = \frac{V_z}{V_{\text{rated}}} \times 100
