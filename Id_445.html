When a battery bank is arranged in a combination of series and parallel, the total voltage and capacity are determined by how the "strings" are connected.

Understanding the Arrangement You have 6 batteries in total, organized as:

• Two parallel banks (or strings).

• Each bank consists of 3 batteries in series.

1.   Step-by-Step Calculation Step A: Calculate the voltage of one series string When batteries are connected in series, their voltages add together, but the capacity (Ah) remains the same.

• Voltage of one string = 6 V+6 V+6 V=18 V6\text{ V} + 6\text{ V} + 6\text{ V} = \mathbf{18\text{ V}}

• Capacity of one string = 40 Ah40\text{ Ah}

2.   Step B: Calculate the output of the parallel banks When strings are connected in parallel, the voltage stays the same as one string, but the capacities (Ah) add together.

• Output Voltage = Voltage of one string = 18 V\mathbf{18\text{ V}}

• Total Capacity = 40 Ah+40 Ah=80 Ah40\text{ Ah} + 40\text{ Ah} = \mathbf{80\text{ Ah}}

Final Result When the batteries are fully charged, the output voltage will be 18 V.

The total energy capacity of this bank would be 18 V×80 Ah=1,440 Watt-hours18\text{ V} \times 80\text{ Ah} = 1,440\text{ Watt-hours}.
