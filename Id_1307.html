the Red Seal (and most provincial exams) will focus heavily on the current Canadian Electrical Code (CEC), specifically the 2024 Edition.

Since 2018, the grounding rules in Section 10 have been simplified, but they can be a "trap" for those used to the old rules where the ground wire size changed based on the service size.

The Modern Rule: Rule 10-114

For a standard 200 A AC service, you are looking for the System Grounding Conductor (the wire running to your ground rods or ground plate).

  • Minimum Size (Copper): #6 AWG
  • Minimum Size (Aluminum): #4 AWG

Key Distinction: Grounding vs. Bonding

On the Red Seal exam, they love to trip you up by switching between "grounding" and "bonding."

Term Purpose CEC Rule/Table
Grounding Connects the system to the earth (rods/plates). Rule 10-114 (Fixed at #6 CU)
Bonding Connects non-current-carrying metal parts together. Table 16 (Based on Ampacity)

For a 200 A Service:

  • Your Grounding conductor (to the rod) is #6 AWG.
  • Your Bonding conductor (e.g., to a metal water pipe) must follow Table 16. For a 200 A overcurrent device, Table 16 requires a #6 AWG copper bonding conductor as well.

Red Seal Tip: If the question asks for the minimum size of a "grounding conductor" for any residential service (100 A, 200 A, 400 A), the answer is almost always #6 AWG copper under the current code.
