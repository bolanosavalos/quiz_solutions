The correct and most encompassing answer is Ground Fault.

The condition you described—where one phase drops to 50V50\text{V} and the other two rise to 230V230\text{V} (full line voltage)—is the classic signature of a ground fault on an otherwise ungrounded delta system.

A "partial ground" is essentially a description of a specific type of ground fault, where the path to ground has high resistance.

  • Ground Fault is the general term for any unintended connection between a phase conductor and ground (or an equipment grounding conductor).
  • A reading of 50V50\text{V} (instead of a full 0V0\text{V}) on the faulted phase strongly indicates a high-impedance ground fault (or a partial ground), meaning there is resistance in the path of the fault.

In the context of diagnosing an electrical problem, you would report that the system has a ground fault on the phase reading 50V50\text{V}.
