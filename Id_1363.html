Even if the question doesn't explicitly mention ground wires or wire nuts, the CEC Rule 12-3034 forces us to include them because they occupy physical space that leads to heat buildup.

If you are following the 2024 CEC strictly, here is how the math works for your specific scenario (#14 feed and #16 fixture wires):

1. The Conductor Count (Rule 12-3034)

We have to count every item that takes up space:

  • Insulated Conductors: You have 2 (the Hot and Neutral #14 wires entering the box).
  • Fixture Wires: You have 0. Under Rule 12-3034(1)(b), #16 AWG fixture wires are not counted if they supply a light mounted directly on that box.
  • The Ground (Bonding): You must add 1. Even if it's a bare wire, Rule 12-3034(2)(a) states all bonding conductors count as one conductor of the largest size in the box (#14).
  • Wire Connectors (Wire Nuts): You must add 1. Rule 12-3034(2)(b) requires adding one conductor for every pair of wire nuts. Since you have at least one pair (Hot-to-Black and Neutral-to-White), you add 1 to the count.

Total Count = 4 (2 wires + 1 ground + 1 wire nut allowance).
