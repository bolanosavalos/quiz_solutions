Both STO and STOW are classified as Extra-Hard Usage cords because they lack the "J" (Junior) designation.

In the context of the Canadian Electrical Code (CEC) 2024, the distinction that makes STOW the correct answer for PV interconnection boils down to the environmental protection indicated by the letter "W".

Why the "W" is Required

Under Rule 64-210 (1), flexible cords used for the interconnection of PV modules must meet specific criteria for the harsh conditions of a solar array:

  • Wet Locations: PV arrays are outdoors and exposed to moisture. The "W" rating (Wet/Weather) is mandatory for any cord in this environment.
  • Sunlight (UV) Resistance: Rule 64-210 requires conductors to be sunlight resistant. In the S-series cords (SVT, STO, STOW), the "W" designation also covers the sunlight resistance requirement.
  • Physical Protection: Because PV modules can be subject to movement (especially in tracking systems or during maintenance), the Extra-Hard Usage rating of the "S" series is required over the "SJ" (Junior) series.
