Step 1: Determine Maximum Conductors in Raceway (Table 6)

First, we must determine the maximum number of #14 RW90 conductors allowed in a 3/4 in. EMT raceway.

  • Conduit: 3/4 in. EMT (Trade Size 21).

  • Wire: #14 AWG RW90 (XLPE insulation, 600 V).

  • Calculation:

    • From Table 9G, the 40% fill area of a 3/4 in. EMT is 132.03 mm².
    • From Table 6A, the cross-sectional area of #14 RW90 XLPE (600 V) is approximately 8.87 mm² (Note: RW90 is thicker than T90/THHN).
    • 132.03 mm2÷8.87 mm2≈14.88132 \text{ mm}^2 \div 8.82 \text{ mm}^2 \approx 14.96 conductors.
    • Max Conductors per Raceway = 14.

  • Total Conductors Entering Box:

    • 2 raceways ×\times 14 conductors = 28 insulated conductors.

Step 2: Calculate Box Fill Requirements (Rule 12-3034)

We calculate the total number of "volume units" required in the box. According to Rule 12-3034, we must account for the conductors and the space occupied by wire connectors (marettes).

  1. Insulated Conductors:
    • Count: 28 (each counts as 1 volume unit).
  2. Wire Connectors (Splices):
    • The problem states "All wires are to be spliced in pairs".
    • 28 wires spliced in pairs = 14 splices (14 wire connectors).
    • CEC Rule 12-3034(2)(b): Add the volume of one insulated conductor for every pair of conductor connectors (wire nuts).
    • 14 connectors ÷\div 2 = 7 volume units.
  3. Total Fill Units:
    • 28 (conductors)+7 (connector allowance)=35 units28 \text{ (conductors)} + 7 \text{ (connector allowance)} = \textbf{35 units}.

Using Table 23, we find the volume required per unit.

  • Volume per #14 AWG conductor: 24.6 mL (or 1.5 in³).
  • Total Required Volume:
    • 35 units×24.6 mL=861 mL35 \text{ units} \times 24.6 \text{ mL} = \textbf{861 mL}.
