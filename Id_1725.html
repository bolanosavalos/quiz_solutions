Here is a breakdown of what each symbol represents in the context of power electronics:

Component Identification

  • A: DIAC (Diode for Alternating Current) – A bidirectional trigger diode that lacks a gate terminal. It conducts current only after its breakdown voltage has been exceeded.
  • B: TRIAC (Triode for Alternating Current) – This is the correct symbol. It consists of two SCRs connected in inverse-parallel with a common gate. It can conduct current in either direction when triggered, making it ideal for AC light dimmers and motor speed controls.
  • C: SCR (Silicon Controlled Rectifier) – A unidirectional device (a thyristor) that conducts current in only one direction once triggered by the gate.
  • D: UJT (Unijunction Transistor) – Often used in pulse generation and trigger circuits for thyristors like the SCR or TRIAC.

Key Features of a TRIAC Symbol

You can easily identify the TRIAC by looking for two triangles pointing in opposite directions (representing bidirectional flow) with a third line (the gate) attached to the side.
